<p><p>Pathological microscopes refer to a range of microscopes used in the field of pathology to study and analyze specimens. Electron microscopes use a beam of electrons to produce high-resolution images, while optical microscopes use visible light and lenses. Compound microscopes have multiple lenses for increased magnification, while digital microscopes produce images that can be viewed on a computer. Fluorescence microscopes use specific wavelengths of light to visualize fluorescent-labeled molecules. Other types include inverted microscopes, phase contrast microscopes, and polarizing microscopes, each suitable for specific applications in pathology.</p></p>
