<p><p>Conductivity sensors are used to measure the conductivity of liquids or solutions. There are two main types: inductive conductivity sensors and electrodeless conductivity sensors. </p><p>Inductive conductivity sensors work based on the principle of electromagnetic induction. They use an electromagnetic field to measure the conductivity of a liquid or solution.</p><p>Electrodeless conductivity sensors, on the other hand, do not require direct contact with the liquid. They use a non-invasive method to measure conductivity, making them suitable for applications where cross-contamination is a concern. They are often used in industries such as water treatment and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
